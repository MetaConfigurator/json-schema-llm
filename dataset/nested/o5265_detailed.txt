The schema describes the representation of a fluid medium. It is an object type. The main properties of the schema are: 'Name' is a string A unique identifier of the fluid medium. It is required. 'Speed of sound' is a number The speed of sound in the medium. Unit: [m/s]. It is required and must be greater than 0. 'Acoustic attenuation' is either a number or a string The attenuation of an acoustic wave in the medium. It can also be an equation string with parameter 'T' for temperature in [K]. Unit: [Np/m]. It is required and may be a non-negative number or a string. 'Density' is either a number or a string The amount of mass per volume. It can also be an equation string with parameter 'T' for temperature in [K]. Unit: [kg/(m^3)]. It is required and must be a positive number or a string. 'Heat absorption fraction' is a number The fraction of the heat that contributes to a temperature increase. Unit: [-]. It is required and must be between 0 and 1 inclusive. 'Isobaric mass heat capacity' is a number The ratio of the heat added to (or removed from) an object to the resulting temperature change per unit mass at a constant pressure. Unit: [(m^2)/(K s^2)] or, more familiar, [J/(kg K)]. It is required and must be greater than 0. 'Thermal conductivity' is a number The property of a material to conduct heat evaluated in terms of Fourier's Law for heat conduction. Unit: [(kg m)/(s^3 K)] or, more familiar, [(W m)/K]. It is required and must be greater than 0. 'Atmospheric pressure freezing point' is a number The temperature at 1 atm at which the medium changes to the solid phase. Unit: [K]. It is optional and must be greater than 0. No additional properties are allowed at the root level.
